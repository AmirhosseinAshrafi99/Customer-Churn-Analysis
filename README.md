# Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behavior using the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset. The objective is to identify the factors driving customer attrition and provide actionable business recommendations to improve customer retention.

## Dataset

* Dataset: IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset
* Records: 7,043 customers
* Features: 33 variables

## Business Questions

* What is the overall churn rate?
* Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
* How do contract types affect churn?
* How does internet service influence churn?
* Which payment methods are associated with higher churn?
* What are the primary reasons customers leave?
* What customer characteristics indicate higher retention risk?

## Key Findings

### Overall Churn Rate

* Churn Rate: 26.54%

### Contract Type

* Month-to-month customers: 42.71% churn rate
* One-year customers: 11.27% churn rate
* Two-year customers: 2.83% churn rate

### Internet Service

* Fiber optic customers: 41.89% churn rate
* DSL customers: 18.96% churn rate
* No internet service: 7.40% churn rate

### Payment Method

* Electronic check users: 45.29% churn rate
* Automatic payment users: approximately 16% churn rate

### Customer Characteristics

* Senior citizens: 41.68% churn rate
* Customers without partners: 32.96% churn rate
* Customers without dependents: 32.55% churn rate
* Customers within their first year: 47.44% churn rate

### High-Risk Segment

Month-to-month Fiber Optic customers exhibited the highest churn rate in the dataset at 54.61%.

## Business Recommendations

1. Encourage migration toward long-term contracts.
2. Improve Fiber Optic customer experience and pricing strategies.
3. Increase adoption of automatic payment methods.
4. Implement targeted retention campaigns for new customers.
5. Improve customer support quality and responsiveness.
6. Focus retention initiatives on high-risk customer segments.
